Every HTML elements on a webpage is a box and CSS came with margins, padding, and border to make the boxes easier to understand. The CSS Box Model is made up of the content itself, padding, border and margin. The content is whatever is in your tag e.g the text you typed in your p tag. The padding is the space between the content and the border. The border is the box itself, the rectangular or maybe square surrounding the content and padding. The margin is the outermost one. It is the space between an element and other elements on the webpage. Our tutor gave an example which helped me in grasping box model well and i will cite it here too. For example, the content is a house in the middle of a big, fenced compound. The content is the house itself. Padding is the space outside the house but within the compound. The border is the fence of the compound. The margin is the space outside the fence. It is the distance between your compound and other compounds.
